Secondary Research

My background is not in brain research, so I feel quite susceptible to overgeneralizing research findings, making naïve assumptions, or being subject to other biases. I am interested in making relevant corrections as I attempt to translate these learnings into lay language and apply them to software designs. If you have corrections or can suggest additional relevant research, please send these to alan@mindfulprogression.com

The brain is a marvelously complex instrument and brain research, while making tremendous strides, is still in its relative infancy. The brain uses 20% of the bodies blood supply and 20% of the oxygen, despite it only accounting for 2% of the total mass of the body. The brain contains more than 100 billion neurons and each connects with between 1000 and 10,000 other cells. The size of this network of nerve cells is staggering and many scientists have labored long in laboratories to give birth to our current understandings of this structure. Tremendous advances have been made possible by FMRI and other imaging technologies to help researchers map reactions and behaviors to structures in the brain.

Habit Formation
  • Habit Formation for a healthy daily habit - Based on self-reported user ratings, the strength of an added healthy habit grew quickly then plateaued at an average of 66 days (following an asymptotic curve).
    • However, the range users reached 95% of their asymptote, was from 18-254 days, highlighting the immense variability between subjects to solidify their habits.
    • Habits involving simpler actions were generally built more quickly than complex ones.
    • Habit formation was not appreciably impacted by missing a day
    • Extrensic rewards were not provided, but the subjects were allowed to select the habit to build
    • Source: Lally, P., van Jaarsveld, C. H. M., Potts, H. W. W. and Wardle, J. (2010), How are habits formed: Modelling habit formation in the real world. Eur. J. Soc. Psychol., 40: 998–1009. doi: 10.1002/ejsp.674
  • Habit Formation for exercise - 4 workouts a week for six weeks was the minimum effort required to build a successful new gym-based workout habit. To form the habit, workouts need to be (ranked with most important criteria first):
    • consistently performed
    • simple and easy to understand
    • fun
    Source: Exercise habit formation in new gym members: a longitudinal study. Journal of Behavioral Medicine August 2015, Volume 38, Issue 4, pp 652-663
