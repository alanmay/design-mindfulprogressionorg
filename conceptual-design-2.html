Conceptual Design - Concept 2

Domain-Aware Opinionated Non-Technological Solution

Solution Description

The user is guided through a sequence of tasks when they first wake up. This solution presupposes that the user is willing to be guided through a sequence of tasks to build positive habits. The list and order of tasks has been predetermined by a behavior designer, based on scientific research and other pragramatic considerations. The difficulty of tasks starts very simple and progressively increases in difficulty as the days go by. For example, to begin building a habit of working out in the morning, the related task the first week may only involve putting on one's running shoes.

The user is given two large decks of cards, similar to the card decks you find in the board game "Trivial Pursuit". Similar to "Trivial Pursuit", the user would take a card from the front of the deck, do the task described then place the card at the back of the deck to preserve the relative order.

Both decks are filled with cards with a similar format. There are daily summary cards and individual task cards.

An additional document (or a series of videos on a website or dvd) is needed to teach the user how to do the non-obvious habits, such as how to meditate and how to do various exercises properly.

Considerations

Pros
  • No technology required - widest possible market
  • Physical reminder as additional supportive trigger to action
  • Provides gradual skill building (consistent with BJ Fogg's behavior model)
Cons
  • The user isn't given a choice on which habits they wish to build
  • It is difficult and impractical (but not impossible) for the user to add or remove tasks.
  • There is no adaptivity or supportive for user lapses. The user only proceeds with the progression when he resumes. For tasks like a workout, this might be difficult given losses in strength
  • The variety is limited to the number of cards.
  • There is no tuning by the designer possible to a released deck of cards.
  • Limited capability for solution to provide positive feedback (perhaps occasion additional cards with congratulatory notes or inspirational quotes)
  • Environmental impact of paper required for printing the materials
