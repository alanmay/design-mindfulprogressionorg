Needs Finding

Emerging Story

Across the research done to date, it appears that a majority of respondents are quite interested in tools to support them with the goal of adding new behaviors into their daily routines( genders and ages). Respondents were motivated by improving their health, reducing stress, and improving themselves, but they needed to see fast results. They were most interested in getting fit and eating right, but reported having been hampered by lack of time, motivation, and responsibilities. Other challenges were trouble with maintaining consistency in their routines, and with getting nearly two hours less sleep per night than recommended. Participants were open to a routine up to about 40 minutes in duration, so time efficiency will be at a premium. They largely prefer to work on this as an early morning activity.

Initial Interest Survey

Background

As a first step in this project, this is a initial exploration of issues surrounding what a morning routine can be.

Goals

  1. Put out a feeler for if help with positive habit or routine formation is of interest to respondents
  2. Understand basic demographics of respondents
  3. Get information on what respondents have already incorporated into their routines and begin to explore what challenges they have and what habits they would like to add.

Constraints:

  • $0 budget
  • Given respondents are not going to be paid, this survey should be extremely short with low cognitive load required.

Methodology

This research method was selected to solicit opinions from a reasonably large number of participants to get some insight on whether people wanted help with behaviors or not.
  • Survey recruitment will be done through austin.craigslist.org's site.
  • Survey delivery will be through a free SurveyMonkey.com's website. This version of SurveyMonkey has a limitation that doesn't prevent multiple submissions, but this is not expected to be an issue, because there is no significant incentive to do so.

Initial Interest Survey Questions and Responses
  • There were 37 respondents when the survey was closed on February 2, 2016.
  • Respondents were not paid for their efforts. I am grateful for their participation and contribution to this project.
  • Respondents were >86% female. This raises an interesting question as to whether this was due to women having more of an interest in volunteering - or if they had more of an interest in the topic itself. The group of respondents had a wide age range.
  • Many survey respondents are open to participating in providing additional feedback on design concepts and early software implementations.
  • Question 7 was problematic. The question asked the respondents to rank habit options in priority order. This structure of this question generates more cognitive load than the others and the user interface on some browser platforms did not enforce adequate consistency checks.
  • Open-ended questions had to have HIPAA Privacy Rule related warnings because email contact information was collected and respondents potentially could disclose sensitive medical-related data. No respondent's actual responses had sensitive data concerns.
  • On reflection, questions 5 and 6 were phrased in a manner that was too leading in nature, hinting at a potential solution.

Survey 1

Background

There were signals of initial interest from the initial interest survey respondents. The next survey is here to explore with largely open-ended questions.

Goals

  1. Provide a larger sample from a wider geographic distribution (US-wide vs. Austin only).
  2. Understand basic demographics of respondents
  3. Get information on what respondents have already incorporated into their routines and begin to explore what challenges they have and what habits they would like to add.
  4. Get insight as to when in the day people are most respondents interested in building a routine
  5. Gauge how interested in supporting tools are the respondents?
  6. Drilling into the challenges, understand how well rested are respondents?

Constraints:

  • low budget
  • would like to gain insight with minimal time investment and with a fast access to results

Survey Questions

Survey 1 Questions

Methodology

  • Survey respondent recruitment will be done via Amazon's Mechanical Turk. Filters will be applied to:
    • limit the results to residents of U.S.A. only
    • to ensure the respondents had experience on the Mechanical Turk platform and
    • their work had a high acceptance rate for previous tasks.
  • Research has shown that samples drawn using Mechanical Turk are reasonably representative of US populations but skewing somewhat younger and somewhat more highly educated than the general population. See backing data: Mechanical Turk The New Face of Behavioral Science
  • As a side note: Mechanical Turk's population of users is almost exclusively limited to USA and India, due largely to expedited payment mechanisms available to "Turkers"(paid participants) in those specific countries but not in other countries.
  • Survey Delivery: The survey will be delivered directly on Mechanical Turk

Raw Data(Microsoft Excel Format)

  • The respondent ids have been removed to remove any potential tracability to users to allay any potential HIPAA privacy data concerns related to protected health information (PHI)
  • Additional columns were added to support coding of responses to questions with open ended responses
  • Additional columns were added to support word clouds
  • There were 50 respondents. 49 is the sample size needed for a 95% confidence level with a 14% confidence interval for the population of the US (approximately 319,000,000)
  • 14% is a high margin of error, but given the original budgetary constraints this was an acceptable approximation that yielded some interesting insights
  • Hours of sleep reported: 5.9 for women and 6.6 for men. Sleep data is notoriously problematic. For instance, researchers Diane Lauderdale concluded that people with higher educated, higher paid got more sleep than average individuals, while Dan Hammermesh concluded exactly the opposite. For a fascinating discussion on the sleep measurement, please see "The Economics of Sleep" part 1 and part 2
  • Time and Motivation far outstripped other challenges reported.
  • 62% reported they were "very interested in a free tool to support building habits

    Survey 2

    Summary

    This survey is to gather more open-ended feedback from respondents about what their motivations are to perform new behaviors as part of their day.

    Methodology

    • Participant recruitment followed the same strategy as survey 1.
    • 100 respondents - 96 is the sample size needed for a 95% confidence level with a 10% confidence interval for the population of the US (approximately 319,000,000).
    • 10% is a high margin of error, but given the original budgetary constraints this was an acceptable approximation that yielded some interesting insights
    • To save budget, demographics were not sampled (assuming that the demographics would be reasonably close to the survey 1's)
    • Survey Delivery: The survey was delivered directly on Mechanical Turk
      • Survey 2 Question

    Diary Study Focusing on the First Hour of the Day

    Based on the findings in survey 1, there was a clear preference expressed by the majority of respondents to work on healthy behaviors first thing in the morning. This diary study's goal is to explore environmental factors prior to the introduction of any type of solution. Although using contextual inquiry was considered, I felt respecting people's privacy in such a delicate time of day was more appropriate and a diary study was an agreeable compromise. This method also allows for a longer period of reporting, but suffers from self reporting biases.
